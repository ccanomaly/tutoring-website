Hello! My name is Christopher Ayala. I am a current student teacher in Tacoma, WA in the Bachelor of Science in Mathematics Education (Secondary) program at Western Governors University. I come from a background in education with experience as an instructor at a math learning facility for K-12 students and as a paraeducator at a local elementary school. My time with these employers has enhanced my instructional approaches, refined my mathematical knowledge, and helped me create strategies to work with students at all developmental levels.
Something that I picked up while I was in school and as I taught students is that math is best taught with an intuitive approach. This essentially means that every concept can be taught in a way that can be followed with “common sense.” Even the most perceived difficult topics can be explained in a manner that anyone can understand. And I believe this is the correct way to teaching secondary mathematics as students best retain information when they truly understand what they are learning and what it applies to. Above all, my goal is to stray away from the idea that math is a difficult subject. Instead, it is the practice of building one’s problem-solving skills, a practice that helps in every other discipline. The more students realize this, the more they will disassociate math with difficulty and replace it with joy and understanding.
I also strive to make free resources for students that they can use at anytime, such as videos and web pages that explain concepts as learning should always have a free option for everyone. Every day I try to make something new that I hope students all over the world will one day be able to utilize when they are struggling with math.
